Biography

I am a MPhil student at Hong Kong University of Science and Technology. My research focuses on Human-Computer Interaction and AI Applications, particularly in developing intelligent systems for healthcare and cognitive enhancement.

My journey into Human-Computer Interaction stems from a deep-seated concern about the impact of technology on human well-being. During high school, I witnessed how elite-driven technological advancement and instrumental rationality could lead to human alienation. This observation led me to pursue humanities and social theory during my undergraduate studies, while simultaneously self-learning computer science and minoring in it to bridge the gap between human needs and technological solutions.

Throughout my undergraduate years, I systematically built my foundation in computer science through coursework and self-study, covering key areas from algorithms to machine learning. This technical preparation, combined with my background in humanities, has equipped me with both the technical skills and theoretical framework necessary for conducting meaningful research in Human-Computer Interaction. My extensive experience with algorithm development and practical AI applications has further strengthened my ability to pursue advanced research in this field.

My research interests focus on three main areas where technology can meaningfully enhance human capabilities while preserving human agency:

  • Personalized post-operative care systems that adapt to individual patient needs and recovery patterns
  • Memory augmentation technologies that enhance natural cognitive processes through adaptive AI assistance
  • Social computing solutions that facilitate meaningful human connections while respecting privacy and autonomy
