Biography

As an MPhil student at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST) under the supervision of Prof. Gelin Kan and Prof. Jintai Chen, my research focuses on developing medical AI and cognitive enhancement systems. Drawing from my personal experience with a rare medical condition, I designed a medical image segmentation and detection framework integrating deep learning, active learning, graph neural networks (GNNs), and reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLFH) to address challenges in small-sample annotation and multimodal data fusion in clinical settings.

My academic journey began in high school, where I became acutely aware of the alienation caused by technology-driven, elite-centric advancements that often overshadowed humanistic values. Motivated by this reflection, I pursued humanities during my undergraduate studies at Beijing Normal University (advisor: Prof. Shen Qingli), seeking to bridge the gap between technology and real human needs.

Over time, I actively integrated technical practice into my academic path, focusing on how technology can address meaningful challenges in healthcare. As a long-term sports enthusiast, I developed a strong interest in the value of rehabilitation and recovery training. This personal motivation led me to proactively apply for a research exchange at Hokkaido University, where I focused on exercise rehabilitation for older adults. These experiences have shaped my commitment to advancing medical AI and rehabilitation technologies that truly serve people.

My research interests focus on three main areas where technology can meaningfully enhance human capabilities while preserving human agency:

  • Adaptive Post-Operative Care: Cross-modal fusion (medical imaging + clinical text) using transformer architectures for dynamic recovery prediction
  • AI-Augmented Memory: Active learning frameworks integrating neuroimaging analysis and NLP to enhance cognitive encoding/retrieval
  • Privacy-Aware Social AI: Lightweight homomorphic encryption + context-aware NLP for decentralized health communication
